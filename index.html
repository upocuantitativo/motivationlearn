Dataset Overview

The Open University Learning Analytics Dataset (OULAD) was published by Kuzilek, Hlosta & Zdrahal (2017) and contains data about 32,593 students across 22 module-presentations at the Open University (UK) during 2013–2014. It includes demographics, assessment results, VLE interactions, and final outcomes. Licensed under CC-BY 4.0.

Cross-tabulation Insights
  • Deprivation: Students in the most deprived areas (IMD 0-10%) have 37.2% withdrawal rate vs. 25.9% in the least deprived (90-100%).
  • Education: Post-graduate students achieve 28.1% distinction vs. 4.6% for those with no formal qualifications.
  • Disability: Students with disability show higher withdrawal (39.4% vs. 30.3%) and lower distinction (7.1% vs. 9.5%).
  • Age: Older students (55+) have the highest success rates (61.6% combined pass+distinction).

RL Environment Design

State space: Student profile features + temporal engagement metrics
Action space: Educational interventions (reminders, content difficulty adjustment, feedback type, resource recommendations)
Reward function: Improvement in predicted final_result probability, engagement increase, assessment completion
Agent: PPO / DQN / SAC algorithm trained on virtual clones
